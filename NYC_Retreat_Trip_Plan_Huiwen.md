### Friday, May 8: Manhattan → Dharma Drum Retreat Center

**Check-in window: 4:00–6:00 PM at the retreat center**

**Morning / Early Afternoon**
- Have a good breakfast and lunch at home — meals at the retreat are simple and vegetarian
- Take care of any last-minute tasks (you won't have your phone for the next 2+ days)
- **Text or call anyone who might need you** — let them know you'll be unreachable and give them the DDRC emergency number: **(845) 744-8114**

#### Getting to the Retreat Center

**Option A: Chartered Shuttle (Recommended)**
- **Departs:** Penn Station at **2:00 PM sharp**
- **Cost:** $75 one-way / $150 round-trip
- **Duration:** ~2 hours direct to DDRC
- Select the shuttle during retreat registration
- **Plan:** Take the subway to Penn Station. Arrive by **1:30 PM** to find the shuttle group.
- The shuttle only runs if enough people sign up — DDRC will confirm beforehand

**Option B: Drive Yourself**
- ~90 miles, about 1.5–2 hours via I-87 North (NY Thruway)
- Free parking at the retreat center
- If you have a car, this gives you flexibility on timing
- Tolls: ~$10–15 each way on the Thruway

**Option C: Train + Rideshare**
- NJ Transit from Penn Station → Middletown, NY (transfer at Secaucus), ~2 hours
- Then Uber/Lyft from Middletown to Pine Bush, ~20 min, ~$25–35
- Coach USA bus from Penn Station to Middletown is also ~2 hours (direct with stops)

**At the Retreat Center (4:00–6:00 PM)**
- Check in at reception
- **Turn in your cell phone** — mandatory for all overnight retreatants
- Get your dormitory assignment and set up your bed
- Orientation and evening program begins

*The actual schedule will include 5–7 thirty-minute sitting meditation periods throughout the day.*

## Do You Need to Rent a Car?

**No, if the shuttle runs.** Since you live in Manhattan, the shuttle from Penn Station is the easiest option. You don't need a car for any part of this trip.

If the shuttle doesn't run, consider whether you or anyone in the retreat group can carpool. Renting a car just for the two days would cost ~$50–80/day plus tolls and gas, and you'd need to find parking near your apartment when you return, which can be a hassle. The train-to-Middletown option is a solid backup.

## Packing List

### For the Retreat (Required / Strongly Recommended)

**Bedding (DDRC does NOT provide this)**
- Twin-size bed sheets (fitted + flat)
- Pillowcase
- Blanket (May evenings in upstate NY can still be cool)
- *Or rent bedding from DDRC for $15*

**Clothing — Retreat-Specific Rules**
- Modest, loose, comfortable clothing in **dark or muted colors**
- Elastic-waist pants — no jeans (uncomfortable for long sitting sessions)
- **Long pants only** — no shorts
- Layers for temperature changes (cool mornings, warmer afternoons)
- Long sleeves or a light sweater for meditation hall
- **Do NOT bring:** tank tops, crop tops, bright colors, eye-catching graphic tees, form-fitting yoga pants (unless covered by a long tunic), noisy fabrics (like nylon)
- **Do NOT wear:** cosmetics, perfumes, or colognes

**Footwear**
- Comfortable walking shoes for outdoor trails (walking meditation happens outside)
- Easy slip-on shoes or sandals (all buildings are shoes-off)
- Warm socks for indoors

**Toiletries**
- Bath towel + hand towel
- Toothbrush, toothpaste
- Unscented soap, shampoo, lotion (no fragrances!)
- Razor / shaving supplies if needed
- Hair dryer if needed
- Personal medications, vitamins, supplements

**Documents**
- Health insurance card (you are responsible for any medical expenses during the retreat)
- Retreat registration confirmation (print it — you won't have your phone)

**Optional but Helpful**
- Your own meditation cushion or bench (cushions are provided, but you may prefer your own)
- Earplugs (dormitory sleeping with others)
- Eye mask
- A warm shawl or blanket for meditation sessions

### Do NOT Bring to the Retreat
- Outside food, beverages, alcohol, cigarettes
- Books, journals, diaries
- Laptop, tablet, voice recorder
- Musical instruments
- Alarm clock
- Pets
- **Your cell phone will be collected at check-in** and returned when the retreat ends
