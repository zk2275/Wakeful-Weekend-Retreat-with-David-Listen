## Day-by-Day Detailed Schedule

### Day 1 — Thursday, May 7: Pittsburgh → Manhattan

**Morning**
- Finish packing (see packing list below)
- Aim for a morning or early afternoon flight

**Flight: Pittsburgh (PIT) → New York**
- **Recommended airports to fly into:** Newark (EWR) or LaGuardia (LGA) — both have easy transit to Manhattan
  - EWR → Manhattan Penn Station: ~30 min via NJ Transit / Newark AirTrain
  - LGA → Manhattan: ~40–60 min via LaGuardia Link bus → subway, or taxi/rideshare (~$40–50)
  - JFK → Manhattan: ~60–75 min via AirTrain + LIRR/subway, or taxi (~$70 flat + tolls/tip)
- **Flight time:** ~1 hour 25 minutes
- **Estimated cost:** $60–170 one-way depending on airline (United, Delta, American, JetBlue, Spirit all fly this route)
- **Tip:** Book two separate one-way tickets (PIT→NYC on 5/7, NYC→PIT on 5/12) for the best price flexibility

**Evening**
- Arrive at your friend's place in Manhattan
- Settle in, get dinner together
- Go to bed early — the retreat starts the next afternoon and you want to be rested

### Day 2 — Friday, May 8: Manhattan → Dharma Drum Retreat Center

**Check-in window: 4:00–6:00 PM at the retreat center**

#### Option A: Chartered Shuttle (Recommended)
- **Departs:** Manhattan Penn Station at **2:00 PM sharp**
- **Cost:** $75 one-way, or $150 round-trip
- **Duration:** ~2 hours
- **Arrives:** Directly at Dharma Drum Retreat Center ~4:00 PM
- **How to sign up:** Select the shuttle option during retreat registration
- **Note:** The shuttle only runs if there are enough participants — DDRC will confirm closer to the date
- You and your friend can take the subway to Penn Station together

**Morning / Early Afternoon**
- Sleep in, have a calm morning
- Eat a good lunch before heading out — dinner at the retreat will be provided, but it may be simple/vegetarian
- Arrive at Penn Station by **1:30 PM** to find the shuttle group

#### Option B: Drive (if shuttle doesn't run)
- **Distance:** ~90 miles from Manhattan, about 1.5–2 hours depending on traffic
- **Route:** Take I-87 North (NY Thruway) to Exit 17 (Newburgh), then local roads to Pine Bush
- You would need to rent a car or rideshare with another participant
- **Rental car cost:** ~$50–80/day from Manhattan locations
- Parking is available at the retreat center

#### Option C: Train + Rideshare
- NJ Transit from Penn Station → Middletown, NY (transfer in Secaucus), ~2 hours
- Then Uber/Lyft from Middletown to Pine Bush (~20 min, ~$25–35)

**At the Retreat Center (4:00–6:00 PM)**
- Check in at reception
- **Turn in your cell phone** — this is mandatory for all overnight retreatants. Your emergency contact number for family: **(845) 744-8114**

## Do You Need to Rent a Car?

**Short answer: Probably not, if you take the shuttle.**

| Scenario | Rent a car? |
|----------|-------------|
| Shuttle is available (both ways) | **No** — subway + shuttle covers everything |
| Shuttle is NOT available | **Consider it** — renting for May 8–10 only makes the Pine Bush leg easier |
| You want flexibility for your free day (May 11) | **No** — Manhattan is best navigated by subway, walking, and occasional rideshare |

If you do rent a car, pick it up near Penn Station on the morning of May 8 and return it the evening of May 10. Expect ~$50–80/day plus gas and tolls (~$25 for NY Thruway). Parking in Manhattan is expensive ($30–60/night), so avoid keeping a car there.

## Packing List

### For the Retreat (Required / Strongly Recommended)

**Bedding (they do NOT provide this)**
- Twin-size bed sheets (fitted + flat)
- Pillowcase
- Blanket or sleeping bag (evenings can be cool even in May)
- *Or pay $15 to rent bedding from DDRC*

**Clothing — Retreat-Specific Rules**
- Modest, loose, comfortable clothing in **dark or muted colors**
- Elastic-waist pants (no jeans — they're uncomfortable for long sitting)
- Long pants only — no shorts
- Layers for temperature changes throughout the day
- **No:** tank tops, crop tops, bright colors, eye-catching graphics, form-fitting yoga pants (unless covered by a long tunic), noisy fabrics (nylon)
- **No:** cosmetics, perfumes, or colognes

**Footwear**
- Comfortable walking shoes for outdoor trails (walking meditation is outside)
- Easy slip-on shoes (all buildings are shoes-off)
- Warm socks for indoors

**Toiletries**
- Towels (bath + hand)
- Toothbrush, toothpaste
- Unscented soap, shampoo, lotion
- Razor / shaving cream if needed
- Hair dryer if needed
- Any personal medications, vitamins, supplements

**Documents**
- Health insurance card (you're responsible for any medical expenses)
- Retreat registration confirmation

**Optional**
- Your own meditation cushion or bench (cushions are provided, but you may prefer your own)
- Earplugs (dormitory sleeping)
- Eye mask

### Do NOT Bring to the Retreat
- Outside food, beverages, alcohol, cigarettes
- Books, journals, diaries
- Laptop, tablet, voice recorder
- Musical instruments
- Alarm clock
- Pets
- **Your cell phone will be confiscated at check-in** and returned at the end

### For the NYC Portion (May 7, 11, 12)
- Regular city clothes for your free day and travel days
- Comfortable walking shoes for NYC
- Light jacket or hoodie (May weather in NYC is typically 55–70°F)
- Umbrella (May can be rainy)
- Phone charger, earbuds
- Wallet, ID, credit/debit cards
- Any toiletries your friend may not have
